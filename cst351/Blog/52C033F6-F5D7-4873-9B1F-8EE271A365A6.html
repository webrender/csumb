Example 1:  Valve Software
Example of: Type Family Variations
This site uses a consistant sans-serif font on all pages, with the font in small caps for the links and logo.  The site makes effective use of Bold, Underline, and Small Caps in order to separate site components and provide emphasis when needed.  The result is a simple, stylish site, which clearly conveys the information it intends to.
Example 2:  MTV Online
Example of: Letterspacing
The MTV site is a very “loud” site.  Lots of music, lots of graphics.  The nav and search bar continue this “loud” trend in the site by having very large, very bold, and very close font.   Even the search bar types your entries in all caps (it forces caps).  The site uses strong contrasting colors, to “yell” its information at you.
Example 3:  Sploid (Now Offline)
Example of: Scale
Sploid was an online news site with a unique layout.  It’s an excellent example of scale - Much like a newspaper, the headlines are very large compared to the rest of the type, and in all caps.  The window scales up the content based on how large you have it, so there is always a “wall of text” on your screen, no matter what resolution you ran at.
Example 4:  NASA
Example of: Leading
NASA is basically the U.S. Administration of the Universe Outside of Earth - so it has a lot of information to convey.  By effectively using a narrow leading for the content on the page, Nasa manages to squeeze in a lot of relevant information down below, while still having a large, easy to read navigation panel.

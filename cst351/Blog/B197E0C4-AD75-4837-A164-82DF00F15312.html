Self Assessment 01
 
 
Design Process: The sketching of my designs helped me to transport my ideas from my brain to the computer.  Sometimes, it is difficult to get an idea out onto the computer, because it’s not yet completely figured out in my brain yet.  By sketching onto paper, I can at least get some of the ideas which I have out, to make room to analyze what is wrong with the idea, and where I need to work on it.
 
While I had some trouble on the first go-around establishing visual hierarchy, so did most of the rest of the class, so I wasn’t too worried - I think my revisions helped to clear up the visual portrayal of the works.
 
Technique and Production: I was pretty competent with the ideas involved in this assignment (alignment, proximity, etc.), and also comfortable with the medium being used to create it (Photoshop).  I think that the final solutions are pretty smooth works, and that they convey a sense of their implied meaning through visual layout properly.
 
Reflection: One of the most important ideas I gathered through this lesson was that something does not always need to be on its own to stand out in a design.  A good designer myst make use of the positive AND the negative space, to make elements stand out to the reader, and to bring the readers’ eye to the place they want it, without basically pointing an arrow at the element intended.  The subtlety of visual cues in design is a good way to affect a viewer’s perception of a piece.

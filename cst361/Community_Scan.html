Community Scan: Seaside

  1. 1.Walk through the neighborhood

  2. •Describe who lives there. 
    The city of Seaside is a diverse community, with people of many different income levels and backgrounds.  For the most part, however, the city seems to be home to many middle and lower-class citizens.  Most of the residences around my service site are houses; however, there are also trailer parks and run-down communities in the neighborhood.

  3. •What evidence is there of access to technology?  Technology itself is present all over the community;  satellite dishes line the houses, while everyone in and around my site, even including the younger students, have their own cell phone.  However, access to computers is much more limited, and I was unable to find any places for several blocks which offered public internet access.

  4. •Any symbols that provide a hint to resident’s ethnicities and cultures?  The windows and porches  in the community are pretty bland, but I have seen figurines of Jesus and Mary in windows, implying that many of the people are from Christian descent.

  5. •What type of churches and temples are near by? There are over a half-dozen churches within a half-mile of my site;  these churches range in sect from Baptist, to Catholic, to Presbyterian;  the closest Temple is just under a mile away, and the closest synagogue is several miles.

  6. •What kind of businesses are nearby?  The Boys and Girls club is right next to Fremont Ave., a commercial street in the city.  The stores closest to the club are Micheal’s Grill and Taqueria, the Food Corner Market, and Rod & Ros’ Gas Mart.

  7. •How many people are using or waiting for public transportation? My service site is just a couple of blocks from a regional transit station, the Edgewater Transit Exchange.  A quick glance at the exchange location revealed that public transportation is key in the community, as there were several people waiting for buses there.

  8. •Does the neighborhood seem integrated or segregated in regards to race?  To class? How can you tell?
    From my impressions, the neighborhood consisted of almost entirely hispanic residents; however, this is not to say that the community was not integrated. Those who were of other races were tolerated, if not welcomed into the community, and my experiences with the residents, as a non-native, were pleasant.

  9. 2.Determine invisible boundaries

  10. •Look for the parts of the community that are inaccessible to community members because of physical boundaries or social situations.  In my community scan, there were very few places that were inaccessible to community members.  There is a highway which lines the far west of the neighborhood, but even the highway has a walkway underneath it so that citizens may access the beach.  In general, the entire community seemed very open and public.

  11. 3.Interview three residents of the community

  12. •Mark, Food Corner Market

  13. •Do you interact with technology?  A bit, I use a computer sometimes at home for making emails or looking up the news.

  14. •Who do you think has power in the neighborhood?  I think that our citizens have a lot of power in the neighborhood, and of course the police.  There are a lot of neighborhood watch groups that keep things safe, I think they keep the power in the community so that we don’t need a heavy police presence.

  15. •What are some important issues facing the community?  I think the issues which we face are the same ones of any suburban community. We want good schools for our kids, low taxes - we want our favorite teams to win at sports [chuckles].  There aren’t any really big community issues right now.

  16. •How has the community changed in the past 5,10,20 years?  Well, we saw a big slowdown in the economy around here around the time Ford Ord closed, then a slump in tourists after 9/11.  However, with the university bigger than ever, we are finally getting more people in the neighborhood again.  It’s started to change, though - from smaller families to a more young people and college students.

  17. •What is your vision/dream for the community?  Well, I just like to see my town grow as a whole - I just want to be successful as a business owner, and I want my community to be successful as a whole.

  18. •Robert, Parent

  19. •Do you interact with technology?  Not a lot. I use my cell phone to talk to family, and call back home, but I don’t really use the computer.  Sometimes i’ll turn on the TV at night.

  20. •Who do you think has power in the neighborhood?  I think that the people in government are the ones who have a lot of control in our community. Luckily we have good community representatives, and our lives are good as a result.  I am very happy with the way things are run in our neighborhood.

  21. •What are some important issues facing the community?  Well, our children are the biggest part of our lives, their education and safety is very important to us.

  22. •How has the community changed in the past 5, 10, 20 years? Well, we moved to the city pretty recently, about 4 years ago.  So far the community has been very nice, we haven’t seen much of a change, except for more and more older teens and college students in the area.

  23. •What is your vision/dream for the community?  Well, I see all these kids growing up in the community, and I just hope that they will all succeed.  A lot of them don’t have all the same privileges and lives, and its programs like RISE that help give my kids the advantage.

  24. •Samantha, Parent

  25. •Do you interact with technology?  Yes, me and my kids both have computers.  I use them for pictures, email, chat - sometimes even webcam with my family abroad.

  26. •Who do you think has the power in the neighborhood?  I think that the power belongs to everyone in the neighborhood - we have choices we can make, and our voices are heard by our district representatives

  27. •What are some important issues facing the community?  Well, safety in the community has always been a big concern.  It’s much safer now than it used to be - I feel very safe where I love, but some parts of the city aren’t as safe.

  28. •How has the community changed in the past 5, 10, 20 years?  Well, the community is in a constant state of growth - with CSUMB, and our kids growing up, it seems like there are more people here every day.  I guess we’ve just started to see new things come up recently, as we become more of our own town.

  29. •What is your vision/dream for the community?  My dream for my community is to see a safe, healthy place for my kids to grow up.  With hard work from me and my community, it has become that place, and hopefully will be for my children’s children.
